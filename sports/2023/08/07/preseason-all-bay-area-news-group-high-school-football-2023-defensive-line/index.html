Preseason all-Bay Area News Group high school football 2023: Defensive line

PRESEASON ALL-BAY AREA NEWS GROUP

DEFENSIVE LINE

(Listed in alphabetical order)

Jeremiah Bailey, Moreau Catholic, 6-0, 240, senior

The leader of both the defensive and offensive lines. The strongest player on the team. Student-body president with a 4.0 GPA. First-team all-league in 2022.

Romeo Castillo, Wilcox, 6-3, 245, senior

First-team all-league and honorable mention all-Bay Area News Group last season. Will also play guard for the Chargers this fall.

Delon Craft Jr., Salesian, 6-6, 260, junior

Two-year starter on both the offensive and defensive line. First-team all-TCAL Rock last season. Will be among the team’s leaders.

Nalesoni Fakava, San Mateo, 6-0, 280, senior

Co-defensive lineman of the year in the PAL Ocean and all-BANG honorable mention pick last season. Had 48 tackles, a team-best nine for loss, and two blocked FG attempts in 2022.

Aseli Fangupo, Sacred Heart Prep, 5-10, 270, junior

A two-way player was a second-team all-BANG selection last season as an offensive lineman. Also received first-team all-league recognition.

Three-year varsity starter and first-team all-West Catholic Athletic League selection last season. Plays DL/OL. Has an offer from Weber State. San Jose State has shown interest.

As his coach put it, spends a lot of time in the offensive backfield while playing defense. Lines up at defensive end and on the offensive line. Has a strong nose for the ball..

Matthew Johnson, De La Salle, 6-6, 252, junior

The four-star standout is big and athletic. Has offers from numerous Power 5 schools, including USC, Oregon, Washington, Michigan and Notre Dame.

A dominant player on defense and a strong blocker on the offensive line for a Santa Teresa team that captured section and regional championships last season.

Timo Poloka, Serra, 6-0, 280, senior

Should be a physical presence for one of NorCal’s premier programs. Has an offer from Nevada.

Santana Roberts, Heritage, 6-0, 300, senior

The strongest player on the team, benching pressing more than 400 points, according to his coach. Has an offer from Pacific Oregon.

Jordan Snowden, Benicia, 6-4, 220, senior

Edge pass rusher is generating interest from a handful of schools, including Stanford, Sacramento State and UC Davis. “Should have a big season,” Benicia coach Scott Swartz said.

Benjamin Thurman, Berkeley, 6-4, 210, junior

Lines up as an edge rusher on defense. Also plays wide receiver, which might be his main position. A three-star prospect, per 247Sports, with offers from Oregon State, San Jose State and Nevada.

Naki Tuakoi, Fremont-Oakland, 6-4, 222, senior

One of the top recruits in his class. The Stanford commit lines up as an edge rusher and linebacker. Has 18 offers, including 15 from Power 5 schools.

Kelepi Vete, Tennyson, 6-6, 255, junior

A force on both the defensive and offensive lines. Has a half-dozen offers at last check, Cal, Arizona, Arizona State and Baylor among them.

Jewelous Walls, Pittsburg, 6-3, 225, junior

A four-star prospect, according to 247Sports. Will be a force for the storied Pittsburg program. Has 11 offers, including from USC, Oregon, Washington and Cal. San Jose State also has offered.
