High school football 2023 preview: EBAL Valley Division

East Bay Athletic League Valley Division

(Predicted order of finish)

No. 1: AMADOR VALLEY

Coach: Danny Jones 

2022 record: 5-6

What to expect: Dons’ coach Danny Jones is coming into this season fired up. “The next two years will be my best two teams I’ve had in (my) 8-year tenure,” he wrote in a Bay Area News Group survey asking coaches about their teams. The Dons are returning to the Valley Division after two years in the Mountain. It means no more De La Salle, Clayton Valley and San Ramon Valley during the regular season. Junior QB Tristan Tia “will be one of the best in Northern California over the next 2 years,” Jones wrote. The 6-2, 190-pounder will miss the first five games – all non-league – after transferring from Granada. The schedule is brutal with the first four opponents being El Cerrito, Campolindo, Acalanes and Monte Vista , but Tia will be eligible when league play begins. Seniors Jake Goldsworthy (RB/LB) and Nate Jetter (WR/DB) will take up some of the slack while Tia sits, as they are dangerous from anywhere on the field. The OL averages 280 pounds per player. Jones predicts junior OL/DL Cooper Longmire will be a Division I recruit. Another up-and-comer is sophomore WR/DB Anthony Harrington.

Season opener: Aug. 25 vs. El Cerrito, 7 p.m.

No. 2: DUBLIN

Coach: Brandon Black 

2022 record: 6-4

What to expect: If defense wins in football, then watch out for the Gaels. They return two all-league LBs in Troy Matheny and John Ortiz. The secondary also is experienced with CBs Rionne Simpson and Jonah Dixon leading the way. The DL is young, but nose guard Nehemiah Suga could help remedy that if he is recovered from injuries that caused him to miss most of last season. “Our defense will be quick, strong and athletic,” Dublin coach Brandon Black said. WR Garrett Esomonu will be a key on offense. He goes 6-1, 170 and is fast. He caught 24 passes for 561 yards in nine games last season and scored five touchdowns. Senior Elijah McDaniel will be in the slot after missing the 2022 season with a broken leg. Junior Jack Duncan takes over at QB after transferring from Amador Valley. “He can run the ball,” Black said. “He makes plays with his feet.” Senior Sean Collins is the backup. He appeared in six games last season, throwing for 741 yards and seven TDs without an interception.

Season opener: Aug. 25 vs. Washington-Fremont, 7 p.m.

No. 3: GRANADA

Coach: Marc Moses 

2022 record: 4-7

What to expect: The Matadors won’t have to wait long to find out what kind of team they have. Three of their first four games are against Monte Vista, Heritage and Berkeley. They are set at quarterback with senior Quinn Boyd. The 6-4, 195-pounder passed for 1,758 yards and 13 TDs last year. He has Christian Clouser to throw to. Both Clouser and Boyd were first-team all-league last fall. The running game features two good-sized backs in juniors Peyton Richards (6-3, 190) and Estevan Duarte (6-0, 195). Gabe Moncada, Dawson Kerezsi and Cal Duarte give Granada decent size up front. All weigh at least 250 pounds. DBs Landon Datillo and Jacob Sanchez lead the way on defense. K/P Brandon Niemeyer was 5 for 6 in field goals with a long of 53 yards last season. He converted all 24 of his extra-point attempts.

Season opener: Aug. 25 vs. Monte Vista, 7 p.m.

No. 4: LIVERMORE

Coach: John Wade 

2022 record: 8-3

What to expect: Coach John Wade will spend the early portion of the season breaking in a couple of young quarterbacks. Junior Matthew Benson and sophomore Matteo Lortie are battling for the starting job in a competition that could last through the non-league portion of the schedule. “Both can throw, but they are very young as far as experience,” Wade said. “Hopefully, the run game can take some pressure off the quarterbacks.” Junior Kamarri Robinson served in a backup role last season. Livermore does have a pair of solid receivers in seniors Joel Indrebo and Justin Matchett. Both played on the varsity in 2022. The star for the Cowboys will be senior OT Xavier Thorpe. The 6-5, 270-pounder is a three-star recruit with several offers including Washington State. “He’s a really athletic, large kid,” Wade said. “He’s as fast as almost anyone on our team.” 

Season opener: Aug. 25 vs. Hillsdale, 7 p.m.

No. 5: DOUGHERTY VALLEY

Coach: Aaron Speaks 

2022 record: 6-4

What to expect: The Wildcats are coming off back-to-back winning seasons for the first time in school history. The trouble is, they are 11-0 in non-league play and 0-8 versus EBAL opponents. They have lost 16 straight in league play and are 1-34 since winning twice in 2015. Former line coach Aaron Speaks will take over as the interim head man after Roberto Clemente went to Milpitas as an assistant. “It’s a great opportunity to lead this team into a new era,” Speaks said. He’ll have to do so without RB Devin Garcia, his top player. Garcia broke his ankle and is out for the season. He had over 1,000 all-purpose yards last year. LB Joseph Halim, the team’s leading tackler in 2022, will start the season on the injured list while he recovers from a foot injury suffered in summer camp. Speaks does have a pair of solid seniors in WR/CB Gavin Bergstedt and 6-3, 255-pound DE Erimi Oshewa to lead the young Wildcats. A pair of juniors, QB Tyler Robbins (6-4, 185) and MLB Ali Wassie offer promise for the future.

Season opener: Aug. 25 at Milpitas, 7 p.m.
