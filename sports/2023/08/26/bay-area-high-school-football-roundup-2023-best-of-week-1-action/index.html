Bay Area high school football roundup 2023: Best of Week 1 action

Welcome back to the Bay Area News Group’s high school football roundup.

Here, you’ll find all the details from the weekend’s action in this news organization’s coverage area, which encompasses teams that play in leagues based primarily in Alameda, Contra Costa, San Mateo and Santa Clara counties.

Check back every Saturday during the season for scores, highlights and top performers, updated throughout the day.

If you have not already, please subscribe here for complete digital access all season long. Your contributions keep us going.

On to the roundup …

Ranked teams

Orange Lutheran 35, No. 2 De La Salle 14

Tied at halftime, De La Salle gave up three unanswered second-half touchdowns and lost at home against an opponent that comes from the same league that features Mater Dei and St. John Bosco. Steve Chavez was the star of stars for Orange Lutheran, rushing for three touchdowns, including one justifiably called “Beast Mode 3.0” by the junior running back’s coaches. Darren Sabedra has the complete story here.

No. 4 Clayton Valley 17, Salinas 0

Playing their first game under new coach Nick Tisa, the Ugly Eagles showed off a stout defense for the entire contest and finally got their offense rolling in the fourth quarter with two touchdowns. The game was scoreless until Will Carranza kicked a 31-yard field goal midway through the third quarter, giving Clayton Valley all the points it would need. Salinas, which was 10-2 last season and beat the Uglies in Concord, threatened to come right back in the fourth period, driving to the CV 30. But Nathan Underwood intercepted a pass to end that drive. The CV offense took control from there, driving 80 yards. Quarterback Mason Lovett capped the drive with a 45-yard touchdown pass to Tony Keck to make it 10-0 Clayton Valley with about nine minutes to go. The Cowboys drove into CV territory on their ensuing possession, but they missed a field goal, and Lovett scampered 5 yards for another TD with a minute left. Tisa has been CV’s co-head coach with Tim Murphy for the last few years, but he hadn’t been the sole head coach since he left Dougherty Valley after the 2017 season. He coached the Wildcats for six years. – Mike Lefkow

No. 5 San Ramon Valley 42, No. 8 St. Ignatius 14

Luke Baker, last season’s Bay Area News Group player of the year, completed 19 of 25 passes for 307 yards in a rout at St. Ignatius. Junior WR Owen Scott had eight receptions for 133 yards, including a 65-yard touchdown. SI’s Pierce St. Geme scored on a 34-yard run to tie the game at seven apiece early, but SRV ran off 21 unanswered points to pull away.  Madeline Kenney was in San Francisco and has the full story.

Helix-La Mesa 45, No. 6 St. Francis 21

St. Francis opened the game with a three-and-out and a quick touchdown on the ground, but Helix responded with 25 unanswered first-half points to roll to a win. Alex Simon was in Mountain View and has the story here.

No. 7 Los Gatos 45, Soquel 14

AJ Minyard had quite the night leading the Wildcats to a season-opening victory at home over Soquel on Thursday. The senior completed 16 of 21 passes for 249 yards and five touchdowns as Los Gatos put the game away in the second half after a competitive opening two quarters. Scott Garwood caught three TD passes and Jaylen Thomas reeled in two. Check out Darren Sabedra’s game story here.

No. 10 Valley Christian 16, No. 9 Wilcox 15

The matchup between CCS powers did not disappoint, with Aaron VanderVeen’s third-quarter field goal for Valley being the game-winner in a defensive struggle. Last year’s WCAL RB of the year Uchenna Eke ran for 54 yards, and four-star WR Kai Hamilton had 48 receiving yards for VC. Tyson Bonilla and Elijah Walker combined to rush for 201 yards for Wilcox. Glenn Reeves has the full report from San Jose.

No. 13 Riordan 36, No. 12 Sacred Heart Prep 17

Riordan scored on its first three possessions and had a 33-3 lead at halftime. Michael Mitchell Jr. threw an 85-yard touchdown pass to Chris Lawson and a 34-yarder to Tyrone Jackson. Freshman Isi Tuugamusu scored on the ground. Sacred Heart Prep got second-half rushing touchdowns from Greg Gamitian and Ravu Savail. Riordan plays at Moreau Catholic on Friday. SHP hosts Sacred Cathedral on Saturday. — Glenn Reeves

No. 14 El Cerrito 14, Amador Valley 7

El Cerrito got an immediate impact from senior Micah Avery when the Clayton Valley transfer scored on a 69-yard run in the first quarter. Senior QB Michael Vanhook added a rushing TD in the second to give El Cerrito a two-score lead. Amador Valley’s Aiden Foley connected with Nate Jetter for a six-yard TD with five minutes to go to cut the Gauchos’ lead to seven. Curtis Pashelka has the full story from Pleasanton.

No. 15 Liberty 49, Enochs-Modesto 3

The Lions led 42-0 at halftime in their season opener on the road Thursday. Jaxon Bell, the younger brother of last year’s star quarterback Nate Bell, had three touchdown runs. Receiver Ryan McKendry caught a 25-yard TD pass and ran the opening kickoff back for six points. Liberty has started each of the past two seasons against the Eagles (1-1) and has outscored them 105-3. Enochs is a relatively new school that began playing football in 2007. It has had only three seasons above .500. Liberty will go from one extreme to the other in quality of opponent next Friday when it kicks off its home schedule against Los Gatos. – Mike Lefkow

No. 16 Campolindo 29, Vanden-Fairfield 23 (2OT)

In a wild game in Fairfield, Joe Revelos caught a 17-yard touchdown pass with 1:29 left to pull Campo (1-0) even. The home team reached the Campo 30 in the final seconds before time expired, sending the game to overtime. In OT, both teams started possessions at the opponent’s 25-yard line rather than the 10. “There was much confusion on the overtime ruling,” Campo coach Kevin Macy said. “It was explained differently a couple of times.” Max Robeson’s 15-yard TD pass to Scott Lyon put Campo on top in OT. But the extra point was blocked. Vanden (1-1) answered with Marcellus Chandler’s touchdown reception but a bobbled snap prevented what would have been the game-ending extra point. Vanden was stopped on the first series of the second OT — one of numerous red-zone stops by Campo’s defense — and the visitors ended the game as Lyon kept Campo’s possession alive with a fourth-down reception to the 8-yard line and Tim Daugherty followed with a “Jet Sweep” run for the walk-off touchdown. “Complete madness,” Macy told the Bay Area News Group. “Our coaches agree that it was the strangest game we have ever been a part of. The defense was incredible.” James Giordani led Campo’s defense with an interception and numerous tackles. — Darren Sabedra

San Marin 27, No. 17 Acalanes 20

The visiting Dons led 14-10 at halftime on a pair of touchdown passes by Sully Bailey, but defending CIF State 4A champ San Marin scored 14 unanswered points in the third quarter and never relinquished the lead. Bailey connected on passes of 13 yards to Henry Souza and 12 yards to Cal-bound Trevor Rogers, both in the second quarter, putting Acalanes on top 14-3. San Marin stormed back to go in front 24-14 after three periods, but the Dons trimmed the lead to 24-20 with four minutes left when Bailey threw his third TD of the night, this one to Paul Kuhner from 26 yards out. After a field goal increased San Marin’s lead by three, Acalanes had one last chance to score, getting the ball back with 1:02 left, but San Marin’s defense held for the hard-fought victory. — Mike Lefkow

No. 23 Archbishop Mitty 20, No. 18 James Logan 12

Junior WR/DB Alec Grimaldo’s 60-yard onside-kick return for a touchdown keyed Mitty’s crazy cross-section victory on the road over James Logan. Mitty’s AJ Cenizal rushed for 63 yards and a touchdown, and Joey Campagna threw for 179 yards and a TD. James Logan quarterback Jonathan Craft threw for two touchdowns and rushed for 73 yards. Joseph Dycus has the full story.

No. 19 Christopher 23, North Monterey County 13

Jaxen Robinson passed for 255 yards and two touchdowns and Amari Bluford, Jaxon Mundorff and Matthew Boles each had an interception on defense as Christopher, playing at home in Gilroy on Thursday, turned back North Monterey County. Bluford was also dynamic on offense, catching six passes for 166 yards and two touchdowns for a team projected to win the Blossom Valley Athletic League Mt. Hamilton Division. The Cougars also got 10 tackles and a sack from strong safety Evan Vernon. “North Monterey County was very tough,” Christopher coach Darren Yafai said. “Big, veteran team. They will win a bunch of games and contend for their league.” Christopher travels to Piedmont Hills next Friday. — Darren Sabedra

Bellarmine 17, No. 20 Menlo-Atherton 14

Bellarmine’s David Diaz-Infante leaned on senior quarterback Parker Threatt to win his first game as the Bells’ coach. Threatt ran for a 23-yard TD, and running back Reece McKeever also rushed for 113 yards on 29 carries. Menlo-Atherton’s 6-foot-7 TE Alek Marshall caught eight passes for 95 yards and one touchdown to lead M-A’s late rally. David Kiefer has the complete story from San Jose.

No. 21 Monte Vista 42, Granada 3

All during the off-season there was speculation Monte Vista would be much-improved after last year’s dreadful 2-8 record under first-year coach Johnny Millard. One game doesn’t make a season, but the Mustangs certainly sent a message with their convincing win over the Matadors and talented QB Quinn Boyd. Three-star RB Julian McMahan ran for 153 yards and two touchdowns, QB Brayden Turner completed 15 of 18 passes for 203 yards and three scores, and receivers Luke Claussen and Brandon Breedlove combined for nine catches. Claussen scored two TDs and Breedlove had six receptions. The hard-driving Mustangs led 28-3 at the half. On the defensive side, LB Talan Jones, a two-star recruit, had nine tackles including two for lost yardage, forced a fumble and recovered a fumble. Millard said Granada moved the ball at times but the Mustangs stiffened up in the red zone to prevent any touchdowns. — Mike Lefkow

Leland 40, No. 24 Half Moon Bay 7

In perhaps the most stunning result of the night, Leland — which went 2-8 last season — went on the road and routed traditionally strong Half Moon Bay. The Chargers scored 14 points in the first quarter and 13 more in the second period to take a 27-0 lead into halftime. The visitors scored the final 13 points in the fourth quarter. Brady Hernandez threw a touchdown pass to Luke Whitson and also ran for a touchdown to lead Leland. The Chargers also got one rushing touchdown apiece from Diego Vasquez, Jack Christensen and Jordan Visperas as they avenged a 35-9 loss at home to Half Moon Bay in 2022. Leland plays host to Palo Alto on Friday. Half Moon Bay returns to the field Thursday at San Mateo. — Darren Sabedra

No. 25 Live Oak 53, Gilroy 0

Luke Newell, in his first varsity start, completed 5 of 6 passes for 119 yards and one touchdown in the rout over traditional rival Gilroy on Thursday. Josh Gagne carried 16 times for 118 yards and two touchdowns. Kieton Dietz had two catches for 68 yards and a TD, and made 10 tackles and an interception playing linebacker, The Acorns are at Mountain View on Friday. Gilroy plays at Carmel on Saturday. — Glenn Reeves

East Bay

Antioch 41, Arroyo 8

The Panthers scored all 41 points in the first half. Antioch’s scoring was fast and furious. Yusef Jenkins got the fun started by returning the opening kickoff for a touchdown. QB Larenzo Mayfield then threw a 25-yard pass to Keon Fosselman to make it 13-0 Panthers. Elijah Stingley had two touchdown runs including one of 35 yards, Mayfield also had a TD run and Roshaud Bartlow scooped up a fumble and took it to the house. The entire second half was played with a running clock. — Mike Lefkow

College Park 14, Fremont-Oakland 0

Host Fremont forfeited in the second quarter because of fights between spectators. Before the game was called, College Park coach Casey Moreno said RB Hunter Pelayo and QB Jimmy Voorhies were playing well for College Park. Four-star linebacker Naki Tuakoi had eight tackles and a forced fumble for Fremont. College Park travels to Oakmont-Roseville next Friday, and Fremont plays at American Canyon. — Joseph Dycus

Heritage 28, Foothill 25 (OT)

Emanuel Rankins scored from 2 yards out in overtime to lead the Patriots over a valiant Foothill squad. Heritage trailed 22-7 heading into the fourth quarter, the Patriots’ only score to that point being JJ Kinnaird’s 100-yard dash with the game’s opening kickoff. Fourth-quarter touchdowns by Rankins and Kinnaird enabled Heritage to knot the score. Trailing 22-14, quarterback Austin Peters connected with Kinnaird to make it a two-point game, then they hooked up again with the 2-point conversion. Foothill drew first blood in overtime on a 30-yard field goal by Matt Fields that made it 25-22. Heritage then got the ball back for one last shot. Faced with a fourth-and-14 at the Foothill 17-yard line, Peters threw a 15-yard completion to Charlie Blaise that brought the ball to the 2. From there, Rankins ran in the winning score. — Mike Lefkow

Las Lomas 34, Vintage 22

Sophomore QB Dylan Thomas threw three first-half touchdown passes, as the host Knights coasted to a surprisingly easy win. Two of Thomas’ TD tosses went to Roman Mercado. He connected with 6-foot-3 junior wideout Colby Cooley for another six points. Kaden Lagaya scored on a 3-yard run. All four Las Lomas touchdowns came in the first half as the Knights built a 28-7 lead at intermission. Daniel Soccol kicked two second-half field goals to complete the scoring for the winners. The game was never really in doubt, with Vintage scoring a touchdown and 2-point conversion with four minutes left for the final margin. — Mike Lefkow

Miramonte 28, Berkeley 12

Miramonte played three quarterbacks in its season opener as it tries to find a successor for Luke Duncan, now at UCLA. However, coach Jack Schram said Carson Blair was stellar during a third quarter in which the Matadors pulled away from a talented Berkeley team. Berkeley’s defense played well, with BJ Thurman returning an interception for a touchdown and Shawn Brown returning a fumble for a TD. PJ Mataafa led Berkeley with 85 rushing yards. Next Friday, the Matadors (1-0) host Piedmont and Berkeley (0-1) plays at Casa Grande. — Joseph Dycus

Pierce 33, Piedmont 21

In its first game since the program stopped its season midway through the 2022 season, the Highlanders went on the road and gave Pierce a competitive game. Junior QB Markos Lagios connected with WR Sam Shelby for a 60-yard touchdown. Miguel Long also had an interception. Highlander coach Jordan Seiden said the team “just couldn’t sustain anything in offense until late in the game.” The Highlanders, who are playing all road games as their field gets renovated, travel to Miramonte next Friday. — Joseph Dycus

South Bay/Peninsula

Aragon 41, Homestead 13

Sean Hickey threw four touchdown passes to lead Aragon. Three of the scoring passes went to Jalen Scroggins, including a 99-yarder. Amaziah Tanielu and Spencer Unga added rushing touchdowns for the Dons. Aragon led 20-13 at halftime before pulling away. “They gave us problems in the first half,” Aragon coach Steve Sell said. “I was pleased we were able to clean it up in the second half.” Aragon hosts Monterey and Homestead hosts Silver Creek next Friday. — Glenn Reeves

Branham 48, Cupertino 7

Branham built a 22-point lead after the first quarter and cruised to a season-opening victory at home. The Bruins led 42-7 at halftime and finished the scoring with six points in the third period. Jack Lewis completed 15 of 23 passes for 201 yards and two touchdowns and also rushed for 26 yards and three TDs. Elias Antillion added 91 yards and a touchdown in eight carries and caught five passes for 55 yards. Next Friday, Cupertino will play host to Bear Creek-Stockton. Branham has a bye next week, then will be at home to face Soledad on Sept. 8. – Darren Sabedra

Capuchino 20, Burlingame 19

Burlingame built a 16-0 lead in the first half on the strength of two touchdowns by Sam Felton before Capuchino scored two touchdowns in a 30-second span to close out the first half. Eddie Gill‘s 24-yard field goal extended Burlingame’s lead to 19-14 in the third quarter, But Capuchino controlled the clock, limiting Burlingame to two fourth-quarter possessions, and the Mustangs scored the game-winning touchdown on a hook and lateral play. Capuchino returns to action Thursday at South San Francisco. Burlingame plays at Soquel on Friday. — Glenn Reeves

Leigh 28, Lincoln-San Jose 0

Shayan Shariat rushed for 134 yards and three touchdowns in 11 carries and T. J. Donaldson threw for 104 yards and a TD to lead Leigh to a victory at home over Lincoln on Thursday. The Longhorns also got a big game from junior defensive end Jonathan Blanchard, who led the team in tackles and tackles for loss. Lincoln finished with 83 yards passing (all by DeZario Burciaga) and 36 rushing. For Leigh, the win avenged its 16-13 loss at Lincoln last season, which ended with Leigh winning just three games while Lincoln tied for first place in the Blossom Valley Athletic League’s “A” division, the Mt. Hamilton. “Great team effort to beat a good A league team,” Leigh coach Kyle Padia said. Next Friday, Lincoln plays host to San Jose in the Big Bone Game and Leigh will be at home to face Pioneer. — Darren Sabedra

Mt. Pleasant 21, Gunderson 10

Coach Manuel Ramirez’s debut was a success as the Cardinals rallied from an early 7-0 deficit. Terreon Hunt got the scoring started when he raced 75 yards with an interception to give visiting Gunderson a 7-0 lead. But Mt. Pleasant took back the lead on a 5-yard touchdown run by Dareuis Williams and a 17-yard scoring toss from QB Jonathan Rosas to John Paul Meza. Mt. Pleasant senior RB Brayan Ramirez galloped 18 yards to paydirt for the only second-half score. Ramirez was Mt. Pleasant’s defensive coordinator before taking over this season for Frank Lopez. Prior to joining the Cardinals’ staff three years ago, he had spent several seasons coaching at James Lick. — Mike Lefkow

Overfelt 36, San Jose 0

San Jose State commit John Stowers rushed for 136 yards and two touchdowns and had 12 tackles and three tackles for loss to lead Overfelt. Senior QB Gabriel Martinez rushed for 122 yards and three touchdowns, and Saulala Niufe scored one TD to go with 38 rushing yards. Pass rushers Lino Eti and Jermiaha Lara each had two sacks. Overfelt (1-0) hosts Woodside next Friday, while San Jose (0-1) visits Lincoln-San Jose in the Big Bone Game. — Joseph Dycus

Palo Alto 28, Fremont-Sunnyvale 10

Declan Packer threw three first-half touchdown passes, two to Jason Auzenne and one to Jeremiah Fung as Palo Alto won its season opener. “He played nearly flawless,” Palo Alto coach Dave DeGeronimo said of Packer. MLB Joey Kessler played well on defense. Henry Buenrostro led Fremont-Sunnyvale with 52 yards rushing and one TD on 12 carries. Jonathan Delos Santos carried five times for 39 yards. LB Emmanuel Perez made six tackles. Palo Alto plays at Leland and Fremont hosts Westmont on Friday. — Glenn Reeves

Pioneer 17, Santa Teresa 6

Pioneer, playing at home in San Jose, scored 10 first-quarter points and went on to defeat a team that won section and regional championships last season. Jesse Herrera ran for 83 yards and two touchdowns and had seven tackles to help lead the way. The Mustangs also got 11 tackles from Marcus Perez, an interception from Edgar Orozco and a fumble recovery by Geovani Quintero Villalvazo as they beat Santa Teresa for the second year in a row. Next Friday, Pioneer will be at home to face Leigh and Santa Teresa will play host to The King’s Academy. – Darren Sabedra

San Mateo 20, El Camino 7

The Bearcats scored 20 unanswered points Thursday after falling behind 7-0 in the second quarter. Junior Emmanuel Fitzgerald was the workhorse for San Mateo, running for 200 yards and a touchdown on 28 carries. Quarterback Cameron Palma, also a junior, snapped a 7-7 tie on a 9-yard TD run in the third quarter. He finished the night with 73 yards rushing. Senior Cole Mannick also had a TD for the Bearcats, who piled up 346 yards on the ground. El Camino got the scoring started midway through the second quarter when Joseph Conti recovered a San Mateo fumble and took it 55 yards to the house. San Mateo hosts Half Moon Bay next Thursday. El Camino plays at Hillsdale next Friday. – Mike Lefkow

Terra Nova 52, Santa Clara 42

Terra Nova jumped out to a 28-7 lead in the second quarter and forced host Santa Clara to play catchup. RBs Mateo Corona and Zachary Perez stood out in Terra Nova’s Wing-T offense. Jeremiah Karongo scored two touchdowns for Santa Clara on a reception and a kickoff return. Matt Nguyen had two rushing touchdowns. Joseph Ting scored on the ground and Mitchell Pacheco through the air for the Bruins, who play at Monte Vista Christian on Friday. Terra Nova plays its home opener Friday against Lincoln-San Francisco. — Glenn Reeves

Westmont 23, Del Mar 7

Mason Pound rushed for 80 yards, passed for 42 and accounted for two touchdowns as Westmont won a non-league game on the road Thursday. Quarterback Andre Latimore led Del Mar, rushing for 231 of the Dons’ 312 yards and one TD. Joseph Dycus has the complete details here.

Intersectional

Castro Valley 26, Carlmont 23

The Trojans scored three fourth-quarter touchdowns to stun visiting Carlmont. The Scots led 20-6 entering the final period. Castro Valley’s winning points came on a 70-yard touchdown pass from QB Cooper Matthews to WR Anthony Mares with 16 seconds to go and Carlmont clinging to a 23-20 advantage. The PAT was blocked, but Carlmont didn’t have time to get in position to attempt a game-tying field goal. Matthews passed for 285 yards and three touchdowns. Linebacker Conner Cooper had 18 tackles to lead the Trojans’ defense. Carlmont QB Carlo Campobello threw for two TDs. Receiver Daniel Mattioli caught four passes fo 74 yards and a touchdown. — Mike Lefkow
