49ers QB Roll Call: Will Brock Purdy sit out against Raiders?

SANTA CLARA — Here is Monday’s practice report on how the 49ers’ quarterbacks looked at training camp:

Purdy’s comeback from elbow surgery gets its first test this week beyond facing the 49ers’ defense. He’ll go against the Raiders, albeit in joint practices Thursday and Friday in Las Vegas rather than any likely cameo in Sunday’s preseason opener.

“I doubt Brock plays (Sunday),” coach Kyle Shanahan said after Monday’s sweltering two-hour session.

Purdy’s work after Tuesday’s send-off practice will be to excel in joint practices, which Shanahan views with much higher regard than preseason games. After all, last year’s joint sessions in Minnesota boosted Purdy’s stock and effectively sealed a roster spot for the draft’s 262nd and final draft pick.

That’s not to say Purdy’s seven practices this training camp have been easy-going; he has rested three practices as part of his arm rehabilitation schedule.

Clelin Ferrell, who signed with the 49ers this offseason, has been rushing Purdy in practice throughout camp, including making ill-advised contact with the QB last week. (Purdy dismissed it as football in a “competing environment.” He praised what he’s seen from the second-year quarterback so far.

“Just to see his mental toughness is something that’s really, really good, especially for a young player,” Ferrell added. “I’ve played against Patrick Mahomes and Justin Herbert, and I would say the one thing about those guys: They never run from confrontation. He enjoys the competition aspect and that’s really, really good to see from a guy that’s really the head of a really well-developed veteran team.”

Ferrell often is coming from Purdy’s blind side as a high-energy rusher against All-Pro left tackle Trent Williams, and he must walk a fine line in when to ease his pursuit while trying to prove his worth after four seasons with the Raiders as a 2019 first-round pick.

“Even if I don’t get to him or if I don’t win on a rush,” Ferrell said. “I try to touch him every play or even jab him in his stomach or touch him just on his back … to let him know I’m right there.”

Purdy completed 9-of-18 passes Monday, one of which was intercepted by Tashaun Gipson Sr. after being tipped by linebacker Dre Greenlaw. Purdy’s best throw came when he found Jauan Jennings at the front right pylon for a touchdown against Ward, who playfully jawed with Jennings earlier in practice and again after that play.

Purdy is expected to practice in Tuesday’s lighter session — Monday’s spanned two hours. Wednesday is a players’ day off, then they face the Raiders in joint practices. A year ago, Purdy used joint practices at Minnesota and the ensuing preseason game to push past Nate Sudfeld on the depth chart, as Shanahan later revealed.

Linebacker Oren Burks has been impressed with Purdy since last year. Burks used to play with Aaron Rodgers and the Green Bay Packers before joining the 49ers last season.

“The poise is very similar, especially coming from a young guy,” Burks said, comparing Purdy to Rodgers. “It’s very rare to see a guy come into a situation like he did last year and handle himself like a pro.

“We’ll see how his career progress but I’m super proud of Brock, even just as a person,” Burks added. “He’s a great human being and person to be around, and people feed off that. The poise in the pocket and the way he leads the huddle, it’s different.”

Darnold went first in the QB2 rotation with Trey Lance, and after nearly having a pass intercepted by Demetrius Flannigan-Fowles, Darnold found Ray-Ray McCloud with a red-zone touchdown pass between defenders A.J. Parker and Ji’Ayir Brown.

Speaking of interceptions, Darnold has not thrown one in full-team drills all camp, which Shanahan initially disputed before reporters insisted otherwise. Darnold entered camp focused on better ball security (55 interceptions in 55 career starts), so Shanahan said “he’s done a good job” avoiding them in 49ers camp. Shanahan added he doesn’t examine practices on a statistical basis, but rather on consistency, accuracy and pass-rush awareness.

“Half these (passes) aren’t getting off because not we’re sacking guys out there,” Shanahan said.

On one play, Darnold scrambled out of the pocket, rolled right and unloaded a pass after he crossed the line of scrimmage; it’s not included in his 5-of-8 passing line.

Lance opened his 3-of-7 practice with an interception, which Flannigan-Fowles made after Austin Bryant provided pressure past right tackle Matt Pryor. Two other misses of note: Parker broke up a pass toward Jennings in the end zone, and a deep shot toward Tay Martin got overthrown.

Lance ran twice among his limited snaps, including once on a red-zone draw. There has not been an emphasis on option keepers as last year’s camp with Lance.

His third-team snaps came in the first hour of practice, and he completed all three of his red-zone passes to running backs after an earlier incompletion to Ty Davis-Price.

–Shanahan is “not at all” concerned about Nick Bosa’s ongoing absence from camp as contract talks continue.

“I just stay out of it,” Shanahan said. “There’s not much to ask. … I don’t know if Bosa has ever done a (full) training camp, and he’s done pretty good. Everyone Wants people here. It doesn’t affect the team much from a defensive end standpoint. I have as much confident in Nick from any player I’ve been around in how he prepares.”

–Wide receiver Brandon Aiyuk did not practice for the first time this camp in what was a well-deserved rest day.

Still not practicing: running back Elijah Mitchell (adductor), guard Aaron Banks (concussion), offensive tackle Jaylon Moore (knee), defensive end Robert Beal Jr. (hamstring) and defensive tackle Kalia Davis (knee).

–Christian McCaffrey began his impressive practice with a touchdown catch against rookie linebacker Jalen Graham in one-on-one drills. Danny Gray had two impressive touchdown catches in one-on-one work.

–Oren Burks remains positioned to take the strong-side linebacker role, formerly occupied by Azeez Al-Shaair.

“I’ve been waiting for this opportunity for a while and it’s here, so I’m focused every day to get better, to put my best stuff on tape for coaches to evaluate,” Burks said. “We have a great offense with a lot of different looks that cause trouble for a lot of defenses, so what better time now than to compete against the best.”
