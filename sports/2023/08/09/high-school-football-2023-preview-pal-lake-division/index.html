High school football 2023 preview: PAL Lake Division

PENINSULA ATHLETIC LEAGUE LAKE DIVISION

(In predicted order of finish)

No. 1 SOUTH SAN FRANCISCO

Coach: Kolone Pua 

2022 record: 6-4

What to expect:  The Warriors made a successful return to varsity football last season under Frank Moro, who has turned the head coaching reins over to Pua, a former player of his. RB/LB Elijah Fields and WR/FS Darren Miller both made a big impact last season as sophomores. WR Payton Jackson and DB Justice Goodman add to the team’s talent at skill positions. The line will be young, led by LT John Langi (6-0, 260), a returning all-league selection.

Season opener: Aug. 25 vs. San Lorenzo Valley, 7 p.m.

No. 2 MONTA VISTA

Coach: Ceazar Agront 

2022 record: 5-5

What to expect: Player turnout is on the increase at Monta Vista, fueling optimism for the upcoming season. Rohit Pamidi returns at quarterback. “He has the speed, IQ and arm ability to lead this team,” Agront said. Sai Patel is a receiving target. Hamza Moharram will be the primary running back, and he averaged 6.6 yards per carry a season ago. Avant Kuchlous is the team’s top lineman and Maaz Zafar, who made four interceptions last year as a junior, is a potential standout in the secondary.  Pamidi, Patel and Zafar are all fourth-year varsity performers.

Season opener: Aug. 26 at Andrew Hill, 2 p.m.

No. 3 CUPERTINO

Coach: Chris Oswald 

2022 record: 3-7

What to expect: Oswald, a 25-year veteran of the high school coaching ranks, says the Pioneers will be fast. Leading the way will be speedy quarterback Shen Hernandez-Ong, who had 190 yards passing and over 100 rushing in his first varsity start last year. “A true dual threat,” Oswald said. James Xue, a wide receiver with big-play capability, will be a primary target for Hernandez-Ong. Liam Radcliffe and Victor Silva are the team’s top linemen. Radcliffe (6-1, 265) is a three-year starter who squats over 550 pounds. Silva is also a three-year starter.

Season opener: Aug. 24 at Branham, 7 p.m. 

No. 4 MILLS

Coach: Rick Angelini

2022 record: 3-7

What to expect: WR Kai Hernandez will play a key role in Mills’ pro set offense. He led the team in rushing last season and was second in receptions. Lopini Vaka (5-10, 260) is a top lineman. Linebacker tandem Ulysses Huerta and Isaac Aqleh are cornerstones of the defense, RB Matthew Juul will take handoffs from Givanni Angelini, a returning starter at quarterback.  

Season opener: Sept. 1 at Kennedy-Richmond, 7 p.m. 

No. 5 SARATOGA

Coach: Stephen Matos 

2022 record: 3-7

What to expect: Despite losing Shane Timmons, the team’s starter at quarterback last season as a junior, who transferred to Los Gatos, optimism is still high at Saratoga. Tristan Hoang and Carter Call, both listed as athletes on the team’s roster, are expected to be two of the top players. G/LB Joey Schoonmaker is a top two-way contributor. Tevita Pahulu (6-3, 300) is a bulwark in the line. Doug Bettinger is another emerging two-way starter at wide receiver and linebacker. 

Season opener: Aug. 25 vs. Prospect, 7 p.m.

No. 6 JEFFERSON

Coach: Amani Stewart

2022 record: 1-9

What to expect: The Grizzlies move down from the Ocean Division with Stewart taking over from Sergio Portela as head coach. Accustomed to a higher level of competition, they have the potential to contend this season in the Lake. Sean Ferreira takes over at quarterback. Wide receivers Michael Slugher and freshman Aaron Gonzales will be targeted on passes from Ferreira. Tre’Sean Barnes will line up at running back. Sophomore center Allan Garcia is a standout on the offensive line and LB Dasan Byrd is a top defensive player. 

Season opener: Aug. 25 at Woodside, 7 p.m.

No. 7 GUNN

Coach: Dethrick Slocum 

2022 record: 0-10

What to expect: Gunn, like Jefferson, is moving down two levels from the Ocean Division to the Lake, which gives realistic hope on improving from a very difficult winless season a year ago. The Titans have size on the line with Tuuta Fisiiahi (5-11, 240) and Mateo Martinez (6-4, 250). Jaden Liu is penciled in at quarterback, JJ Racz at running back, Sam Dellenbach at wide receiver and George Skinner at tight end. Devin Sidhu is expected to make contributions at wide receiver and cornerback as well Cameron Owen at fullback and linebacker.

Season opener: Aug. 25 vs. James Lick, 7 p.m.

No. 8 LYNBROOK

Coach: Jerome Holloway 

2022 record: 1-9

What to expect: Dylan White and sophomore Brandon Zhang are contending for playing time at quarterback. Both have great arms, according to Holloway. White will play linebacker on defense, Zang defensive tackle. Junior Bryan Madueno-Pennington can be utilized as a running back or wide receiver. FB/LB Diego Perez is described as an “old-school, hard-nosed runner and great tackler.” C/DT Jacky Wang is a standout on the line, along with Brandon Knepfler and Inri Garcia. 

Season opener: Aug. 26 vs. Yerba Buena, 7 p.m.
