High school football 2023 preview: TCAL Rock Division

TRI-COUNTY ATHLETIC LEAGUE ROCK DIVISION

(Predicted order of finish)

No. 1 EL CERRITO

Coach: Jacob Rincon

Record in 2022: 13-1

What to expect: It won’t be easy for the Gauchos to duplicate last season’s run to the North Coast Section Division III championship. They reeled off 13 consecutive wins before falling in a regional title game to Grant-Sacramento. But they might have to talent to pull it off. Back from last season’s team is QB Michael Vanhook, a first-team all-league player who obviously knows what it takes to win. “He has had a great off-season on his development and expect great things this year,” coach Jacob Rincon said. WR/DB Kamani Jackson has multiple college offers, as does RB Micah Avery. Rincon said Avery is capable of 1,500 yards and at least 10 TDs. OL/DL Tome Veukiso, now a senior, is a three-year varsity player. He’ll team up on the lines with another standout, senior Salesi Mafuahingano. “Strength of our team is that we have a lot of returners with experience,” Rincon noted.

Season opener: Aug. 25 at Amador Valley, 7 p.m.

No. 2 DE ANZA

Coach: Tyree Reed

Record in 2022: 6-5

What to expect: The Dons beat everyone in the league last season except for El Cerrito. They have experience on the offensive and defensive lines, and a few skill position players are back, too. “We have a group of guys who are bought into what we are trying to achieve,” coach Tyree Reed said. Elijah Benton was a first-team all-league defensive lineman last year. The three-year varsity starter also plays on the offensive line. Davion Dunkley, another returning first-team all-league player, will be counted on to play receiver and defensive back and return kicks. Armani Isom takes over as starting running back, and Victor Gerry will help anchor the offensive line. Junior athlete Desmond Sparks is another player expected to make an impact.  

Season opener: Aug. 26 at  St. Vincent, 1:30 p.m.

No. 3 PINOLE VALLEY

Coach: Troy McConico

Record in 2022: 9-4

What to expect: The Spartans had a magical run last season, following up their win North Coast Section Division VI final with another triumph to capture the Division 7-AA state title. Although 16 players graduated from the historic team, the returning players are aiming to build off what their former teammates achieved. “We have a lot of high-character guys that love to play the game,” coach Troy McConico said. Athlete Tony Thompson is among them. He brings size, speed and a high IQ to the field. “He can play any skill position at a high level,” McConico said. “We expect him to have a great season and also play at the next level.” Maddox Wu, who played QB last season, is moving to RB. “He is a great team leader,” McConico noted. “Great combination of speed and power.”  

Season opener: Aug. 25 vs. American, 7 p.m.

No. 4 SALESIAN

Coach: Chad Nightingale

Record in 2022: 6-6

What to expect: Two years removed from winning 11 games and capturing a state championship, the Pride will field a junior-heavy team that includes QB Marcus Burke and OL/DL Delon Craft, Jr. Both started last season as sophomores. Two more juniors expected to play crucial roles are RB/LB Cahmari Blakely and RB/DB A’Million Morgan. The team’s key seniors are TE/DE Timmy Nguyen, WR/DB Marco Turqueza, RB/DB Luis DeJesus, RB/DB Ethan Alfonzo and RB/LB Anthony Cook. The Pride’s 6-6 finish last season was its first non-winning season since 2017.

Season opener: Aug. 26 vs. Archie Williams, 1 p.m.

No. 5 BETHEL

Coach: Daniel Martinez

Record in 2022: 2-8

What to expect: The Jaguars are aiming for a turnaround after a young team in 2022 went through its share of growing pains. Among the players that will be counted on to lead the way are junior C/DT Chase Toia, senior MLB/RB Darnell Jefferey, senior WR/S Jaden Hurth, junior CB/RB Cameron Usher and junior TE/OLB Damoni Jackson. “Expecting a lot out of this core group,” coach Daniel Martinez said. Bethel won six games in 2021, including three of four in its league, while playing in the TCAL’s lower division, the Stone. The team is entering its second season in the league’s upper division, the Rock.

Season opener: Aug. 25 at Hayward, 7 p.m.

No. 6 HERCULES

Coach: Gerald Montgomery II 

Record in 2022: 0-9

What to expect: After a rough season in 2022 that included numerous forfeits, per results reported to MaxPreps, the Titans hope to start getting back on track under first-year coach Gerald Montgomery II, the son of the man known as “Mr. Hercules,” the late Gerald Montgomery. The new coach said the team’s goal this season is “rebuilding a program back to its standard of play.”  Two years ago, when Hercules was in the TCAL’s Stone Division, the team went unbeaten in league play. The Titans are entering their second year in the stronger Rock Division.

Season opener: Aug. 25 at Encinal, 7 p.m.
